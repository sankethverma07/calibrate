## THE 28-SECOND EVOLUTION

### 0:00 – 0:03 · *Live the still*
Begin on the attached first frame, unchanged. Locked-off camera ~50 cm above

cycle, the horizon glow trembles faintly. No camera motion. Let the still

### 0:03 – 0:07 · *The focal plane sweeps forward*
Camera HOLDS — no pan, no dolly. The focal plane racks from 8 m → 4 m across
four seconds. Background dust defocuses into creamy hex bokeh; midground
particles crystallize into pinpricks. The viewer's eye rides the moving
focal plane through depth. The cyclorama crossfades **Cruising teal → Locked
In blue** over ~3 seconds, the way a stage gel slides into the key. Wave

### 0:07 – 0:10 · *Macro landing*
Camera lifts 10 cm for parallax. Focal plane continues to 2 m. Near particles

### 0:10 – 0:12.5 · *Sliders rise out of the wave*
Camera dollies back and lifts to 1.2 m. From the wave surface, eight thin

### 0:12.5 – 0:15.5 · *One slider drives the ocean* — **SLOW MOTION**

No chrome anywhere.

### 0:15.5 – 0:17.5 · *Bands aligned*

## STYLE CONSTANTS

Cinema-lens aesthetic throughout: hexagonal bokeh (six-blade iris), ACES
filmic color grade, deep blacks at ~5 IRE (never crushed), 6 % film grain,
40 % corner vignette. Motion-control camera smoothness — never hand-held.
Apple-level restraint in every frame.

NO lens-flare streaks. NO chrome reflections on glass. NO clearcoat shine on
the card. NO oversaturated colors (mood saturation max 50 %). NO music-video
cuts — cut only on motion settles or luma matches. NO whip-pans. NO mid-shot
camera shake.

The room always has **air**. The product always has **weight**. The film
grows out of the attached first frame — it does not start over.
