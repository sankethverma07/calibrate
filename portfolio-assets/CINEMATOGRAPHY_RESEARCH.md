# Calibrate · Hero Film Cinematography Research

Compiled research on Apple + Samsung product film signatures, distilled into
a working playbook for the Calibrate 15–20s hero film
(glass EQ card · particle wave field · no audio).

---

## TL;DR — the position to take

**Samsung atmosphere, Apple cadence.** Use Samsung's lineage for the *texture* of
the film (color-wash backdrop, particle atmosphere, off-center composition,
robot-precise camera moves) and Apple's discipline for the *resolution* (3.5–4s
held hero on a still composition, restrained saturation in the final beat, no
drop shadow, true blacks).

This is the strongest position for a desktop-app product film that needs to
feel premium without feeling cold. Apple-only would feel too austere for an
audio product where mood and color *are* the feature. Samsung-only would feel
busy and gaming-adjacent.

# PART 1 · Apple's Visual Grammar

## Repeated cinematographic moves

- **The hero reveal.** Product enters from black, rotates or pushes into frame,
  then settles. Slow, deliberate.
- **The orbit / arc shot ("the Apple shot").** 360° tracking around the product,
  motion-controlled or motorized turntable + locked camera.
- **Macro-to-wide pull.** Starts inside a detail (speaker mesh, lens stack,
  chamfer), pulls back to reveal whole device.
- **Parallax dolly.** Lateral slide across the product against deeper background,
  exaggerating depth without rotation. Vision Pro's signature.
- **Exploded view → assembly.** Components fly apart on staggered timing, hold,
  snap back together.
- **Specular highlight choreography.** A single bright glint travels along an
  edge as the product turns. Animated, not accidental — timed to the camera move.
- **The settle.** Every Apple shot ends on a held, locked composition for
  0.5–1.5s before cut.
- **Floating product in void.** Black/near-black negative space, product
  suspended dead-center or slightly above center, drop shadow absent or barely
  perceptible.

## Lighting

- **3+ backlights** create the sci-fi/high-tech feel — angled to highlight
  edges, not details.
- **5 lights total** in a typical Apple commercial setup (product, background,
  screen reflection).
- **Signature 45° screen reflection** — one light through a diffusion scrim,
  cut at 45° with black card.
- **Color-accurate LED panels with grids** (Aputure Nova / ARRI SkyPanel /
  Litepanels Gemini) — soft but directional, DMX-controlled.
- **Rim lights mandatory on glass and metal** — prevents the "pasted on
  background" look. The secret to cinematic depth.
- **Black backgrounds**: light from below the product, single boom-mounted
  through umbrella, spill kept *off* the backdrop.
- **For glass screens to glow**: the screen content IS the key light.
  Underlight the bezel, let the panel bloom into a soft halo.

## Color signature

- **True blacks, 0–5 IRE.** Apple does not lift shadows.
- **Restrained saturation overall**, ONE accent color per beat.
- **Gentle highlight rolloff.** Apple Log records low contrast / low
  saturation to preserve highlight + shadow detail.
- **Selective desaturation in shadows + highlights**, saturation held in
  midtones — the cinematic curve.
- **Cool overall bias** (subtle teal/blue shadow lift), warm accents only
  for human/lifestyle.
- **Specular highlights slightly desaturated** — chrome reads near-white
  with cool tinge, never warm yellow.

## Pacing

- **Hero stingers: 25–45 seconds total.**
- **Average shot length: 1.2–2.5s in body, anchor shots 3–5s.**
- **Three-act structure:** hero reveal · details · product in context.
- **Hold opening and closing shots 2–4× the average length.**
- **For a 15–20s film:** 8–12 cuts total. Wide-establishing hold (2.5–3.5s)
  → 5–8 detail beats (1–1.5s each) → held hero (3–4s).

## Top 5 Apple shots to reverse-engineer

1. **Vision Pro UI panels parallax-floating** (~0:30) — lateral dolly past
   floating glass panels, depth-aware blur foreground + background. Sells
   "spatial" without any rotation. **For us:** slow lateral parallax with
   wave receding behind card, focus pulled to the card.
2. **iPhone 16 Pro macro push then explosion** (0–4s) — probe-lens push into
   lens stack, hold, components fly out radially. **For us:** push into the
   card's EQ-band glow, hold on canvas texture, pull back as wave responds.
3. **AirPods Pro 2 single-glint chamfer rotation** (~0:08–0:11) — half-second
   orbital, one specular highlight choreographed exactly along the chamfer.
   **For us:** time a single edge highlight to travel the card's bevel during
   a 0.5s nudge-rotation.
4. **AirPods Max material-reveal cuts** — anodized aluminum, mesh fabric,
   polymer, each in 1.2s macro before assembled product. **For us:** cut
   between three macros — glass refraction, edge bevel, internal EQ glow —
   before full reveal.
5. **Spike Jonze HomePod "Welcome Home"** — *the world responds to the
   product*. Environment expands/warps in response to the device. **For us:**
   wave field reacting to the card IS this beat. Frame the card off-center
   (rule of thirds), wave occupies dominant negative space.

## What Apple does NOT do

- No lens flare anamorphic streaks
- No fast hand-held wobble or whip-pans (motion-control smooth always)
- No crushed blacks with visible banding (add 1–2% film grain)
- No drop shadows under floating products
- No oversaturated accent colors (max ~30–50% saturation)
- No locked-symmetrical-still-for-the-whole-film (always subtle parallax,
  breathing, specular travel)
- No abrupt cuts on motion peaks — cut on *settles* or luma matches
- No music-video maximalism
- No flat PNG floating in void — rebuild UI as 3D with real material response

# PART 2 · Samsung's Visual Grammar

## Repeated cinematographic moves

1. **"Infinite void" cyclorama** — depthless white/grey stage. Product floats
   without spatial anchors; the product IS the scene's geometry.
2. **Bolt-arm motion control** — 6-axis cinema robot (MRMC Bolt), 12 m/s,
   180° rotation in one second. Every Samsung hero shot from 2023 onward has
   motion no human operator could replicate.
3. **Unfold-as-camera-move** — hinge action choreographed across multiple
   frame sizes: tight macro on crease → synchronized push-out as device opens
   → snap to wide hero as screen ignites.
4. **Triangular / multi-instance framing** — multiplied product, off-axis
   orbital symmetry, mirrored hero pairs.
5. **Materializing-from-components reveal** — exploded-view CG reveals where
   the product assembles from its parts. Apple shows finished objects;
   Samsung shows assembly.
6. **Trompe-l'œil + projection-mapped composites** — Samsung doesn't hide
   the seam between real and CG; they embrace the blend.
7. **Reactive lighting** — practical LED tracks shift hue across the cyc
   synced to the camera move, producing the "color wash follows the hero"
   look.

## Lighting

- **Soft, large, wraparound key + brand-color rim.** Near-shadowless ambient
  bed + directional steel-blue (or mood-color) rim to separate product from
  same-luminance backdrop.
- **Dual rim on glass/ceramic** — two strip softboxes raked across glass at
  70–80° from camera axis. Samsung exaggerates via post-bloom.
- **Reflective floor + sky-dome wash** — black gloss floor catches gradient
  backdrop, doubling color real estate.
- **Single-source dramatic toplight for "ceremony" beats** — theatrical
  shadow halo on floor.

## Color signature

| Dimension | Samsung | Apple |
|---|---|---|
| Saturation | High; pushed primaries | Restrained; muted |
| Backdrop | Gradient washes (purple→amber, pink→teal) | Flat white/grey/single matte |
| Black point | Lifted on hero stage; true on product | Crushed |
| Skin tones | Cooler highlights, warm mids | Naturalistic |

Samsung's signature complementary palettes:
- Purple `#6A2BBF` + amber `#FF9A3C`
- Teal `#1FB6B0` + magenta `#E946A3`
- Electric blue `#2D6BFF` + warm white

## Pacing

- **Average shot length 2.0–2.5s in Galaxy Unpacked intros.**
- **Contrast pacing**: rapid micro-cuts (0.4–0.7s flash-cuts) at the
  transformation moment, then long held hero (3.5–5s).
- For a 15–20s no-audio loop: 6–8 shots, ~2.2s avg, one acceleration burst
  (3 cuts in ~1.2s) at the card-locks-in moment, then 4s hero hold.

## Top 5 Samsung shots to reverse-engineer

1. **Z Fold7 "infinite void" hero orbit** — Bolt-arm 270° orbit around device
   on white cyc, focus locked, soft steel rim. **For us:** orbit the card
   90–180° while particle field rotates the opposite direction (counter-
   rotation reads as cinematic depth).
2. **S25 Ultra "exploded materialize"** — components fly together CG-style,
   resolve into device. **For us:** EQ sliders materialize one at a time as
   particle ribbons condensing into bars on the glass.
3. **Galaxy Ring three-angle parallax stack** — three rings at three rotation
   angles, foreground/midground/background, hero deep with shallow DOF,
   focus pull racks through them. **For us:** two ghost EQ cards in
   background at lower opacity, rack focus from background ghosts to hero.
4. **Z Flip5 unfold reveal** — device arrives closed/dark, screen ignites.
   **For us:** card arrives closed/dark, particle field "writes" the EQ
   curve onto its face — glass goes from passive to live instrument.
5. **S24 "Circle to Search"** — gesture → light → result. **For us:** mood
   selection emits a light pulse tracing the EQ curve and changing the
   particle color wash. Cause-and-effect is the hero, not the chrome.

# PART 3 · Apple vs Samsung — Conscious Lineage Map

| Dimension | Apple | Samsung |
|---|---|---|
| Lighting | Daylight-soft, key+minimal fill | Soft key + branded rim + reactive washes |
| Backdrop | Flat white/deep grey/matte | Saturated gradient; complementary pairs; reflective floor |
| Camera | Slow dolly, tripod-locked | Bolt-arm; perfect orbits; whip-snap decelerations |
| ASL | 3.5–4.5s, metronomic | 2.0–2.5s + flash-cut burst + long hero hold |
| Product treatment | Finished object presented | Materializes from parts; multiplies; unfolds |
| CG visibility | Hidden; sells as real | Embraced; CG/real composite openly |
| Color | Restrained, low chroma | High chroma, complementary |
| Atmosphere | Negative space, silence | Particles, rays, color sprays |
| Hero pose | Centered, symmetrical | Off-center, dutch tilts, multiplied |

# PART 4 · The Calibrate Shot List

A working storyboard for a 16-second film. Eight shots, ~2s average, with
Samsung's acceleration burst at the mood-shift beat and Apple's 4s held
hero to close.

### Shot 1 · 0:00–0:03 · MACRO BOKEH OPENING
- Particle wave field in extreme close-up, all bokeh, no recognizable
  subject yet. Mood color: cruising teal at ~25% saturation.
- Camera deep in the wave plane, focus floats, no anchor point.
- ~3s hold. Builds the "what is this?" question.

### Shot 2 · 0:03–0:05 · FOCUS RACK PULL
- Camera pulls back. Focal plane sweeps from foreground bokeh to the
  middle distance.
- Particle band starts to resolve into a horizontal floor.
- Card is still off-frame.

### Shot 3 · 0:05–0:07 · CARD ENTERS — OFF-CENTER, BACKLIT
- Glass card slides into upper-right third of frame, backlit by a
  soft mood-color rim. Edge bevel catches a single specular highlight.
- Slight parallax dolly left → right as the card arrives.

### Shot 4 · 0:07–0:08.5 · DETAIL: EDGE BEVEL MACRO (Apple beat)
- Cut to extreme close-up of the card's edge. The orange handle of one
  EQ slider catches the rim light. Glass refraction visible.
- ~1.5s hold.

### Shot 5 · 0:08.5–0:09.5 · DETAIL: SLIDER MATERIALIZES
- One slider handle slides up to its EQ value position. Particles in
  the wave below it brighten in response. Cut.

### Shot 6 · 0:09.5–0:10.5 · ACCELERATION BURST (Samsung beat)
- Three rapid micro-cuts in 1 second:
  - 0:09.5 — close on second slider settling
  - 0:09.9 — wide shot, all 8 sliders settling, mood color shifting
    from cruising teal → locked-in blue
  - 0:10.3 — particle field reacting, color wave passing through
- Energy peaks here.

### Shot 7 · 0:10.5–0:12 · LATERAL DOLLY PAST RESOLVED CARD
- Camera dollies left, card holds its position, wave field recedes
  in parallax behind. Bokeh discs visible at frame edges.
- Card is now lit by full mood color (locked-in blue), full UI visible.

### Shot 8 · 0:12–0:16 · HELD HERO (Apple beat)
- Camera locks. Card sits at rule-of-thirds upper-right. Wave fills
  lower 2/3 of frame, gently breathing.
- A single specular highlight slowly travels across the card's top
  bevel over the 4 seconds.
- Subtle breathing pulse on the wave (1 cycle in 4 seconds).
- Color holds at locked-in blue — no further changes.
- Fade-to-black or loop point at 0:16.

# PART 5 · Specific Technical Recipes

### Camera
- **Lens equivalent:** 50mm normal for the lateral dolly, 85mm for
  detail beats (Apple), 24mm wide for the opening bokeh.
- **Aperture:** opens at f/1.4 equivalent (max bokeh) at shot 1, closes
  to f/2.8 by shot 3 (subject sharp, background still blurred), holds
  f/4–f/5.6 for the held hero (deeper DOF for the locked-in resolution).
- **Camera moves:** all motion-control-smooth. No human shake. Bolt-arm
  style precision for shots 2, 3, 7.

### Lighting
- **Card key light:** soft top-key on the card front, simulated via
  HDR environment at low intensity (~0.4–0.6).
- **Card rim light:** mood-tinted backlight from behind the card,
  creates the edge halo. This is animated — color shifts with mood.
- **Wave key:** ambient bottom-lit, very subtle. The wave should feel
  self-illuminated (the particles have height-driven brightness).
- **No floor reflection** — different from Samsung. Calibrate is
  software, the environment is space, not stage.

### Color
- **Background: pure black 0 IRE.** Apple discipline.
- **Wave dust: white with mood tint at 30%.** Not saturated.
- **Card UI: orange handles + white text + dark grey body.** Holds
  the only saturation in frame.
- **Mood color appears in:** card rim light, wave subtle tint, the
  "color wave" that travels through the field on transitions.
- **Saturation envelope:** rises during shot 6 (acceleration burst,
  mood shift = chromatic event), settles back at shot 8.

### Pacing
- Shot lengths: 3 · 2 · 2 · 1.5 · 1 · ~1 (burst of 3) · 1.5 · 4
- Total: 16 seconds
- Cuts on motion settles (Apple discipline)
- Burst cuts (shot 6) are the only place we cut mid-motion (Samsung
  discipline)

### Sound (if added later)
- No audio per the brief, but if music is added: single rising synth
  pad that swells through shots 1–5, brief percussive transient on the
  acceleration burst (shot 6), held tone resolving through shot 8.
