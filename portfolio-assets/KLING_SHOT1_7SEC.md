## WHAT THE FIRST FRAME ESTABLISHES (preserve all of this)

A wide cinematic stage with *air*, never a void. Deep teal cyclorama with
slow vertical light shafts cutting from above through volumetric haze — like
sun-rays through dust in a vast room. A soft horizon glow line sits at the
lower third of frame. Corners fade to deeper teal-indigo for a natural
cinematographic vignette.

In front of this cyclorama lies an **ocean of light** — a wide horizontal
plane of ten thousand microscopic photonic particles. Self-luminous
pinpoints, each the brightness of a distant star. The lens DNA the shot
must honor throughout:

- **In focus**: razor-sharp cool-white pinpoints (5500–7000 K) with a tiny
  four-point diffraction spike — cinema iris artifact.
- **Out of focus**: hexagonal bokeh discs with bright soap-bubble rims and
  faint red/cyan chromatic fringing on the edge — six-blade Cooke S4/i iris.
- A creamy hex-bokeh wall in the foreground, sharp pinpricks in the
  midground, softer falloff again in the distance. Macro-photography
  depth-of-field.

Atmosphere is deeply desaturated and low-luminance (~10–15 %) so the
cyclorama never competes with the wave — but the room *always reads as a
room*. Mood at frame zero: **Cruising — teal `#2dd4bf`**.

## THE 7-SECOND EVOLUTION

### 0:00 – 0:03 · *Live the still*
Begin on the attached first frame, unchanged. Locked-off camera ~50 cm above
the wave surface, tilted down 12°, 50 mm cinema lens at f/2. No camera
motion. The frame *becomes* a video by virtue of its own atmosphere:

- Particles drift gently along the horizontal axis, slow lateral motion.
- The wave plane undulates on a smooth multi-directional sine displacement —
  small amplitude, calm-sea feeling.
- Vertical light shafts breathe on a slow ~6-second cycle, intensity rising
  and falling subtly.
- The horizon glow line trembles faintly at the lower third.
- Hexagonal bokeh discs in the foreground sparkle as their soap-bubble rims
  catch the shifting light shafts. The 4-point diffraction spikes on the
  sharp midground pinpoints scintillate.
- Volumetric haze drifts at a near-imperceptible pace.

Let the still settle. Let it breathe. Let the viewer fall into the world the
image already promised.

### 0:03 – 0:07 · *The focal plane sweeps forward*
Camera HOLDS POSITION. No pan. No dolly. No tilt. The only thing that moves
is the focal plane.

The focal plane racks smoothly from **8 metres → 4 metres** across the four
seconds. As it sweeps:

- Background particles, previously razor-sharp pinpoints, defocus into
  creamy hexagonal bokeh discs with bright soap-bubble rims.
- Midground particles, previously bokeh, crystallize into pixel-precise
  cool-white pinpoints with their tiny 4-point diffraction spikes blooming
  into existence.
- The viewer's eye rides the moving focal plane THROUGH the depth of the
  wave — like a macro photographer racking through a field of dewdrops.

Simultaneously, over ~3 seconds, the cyclorama crossfades from **Cruising
teal `#2dd4bf` → Locked In blue `#60a5fa`** on a smooth lerp — the way a
stage gel slides into the key light. The teal vertical light shafts subtly
shift cooler-bluer in temperature. The horizon glow's tint follows. Wave
amplitude swells very gently as the mood deepens.

The shot ends at 0:07 on the new focal plane settled, the room now reading
slightly cooler and deeper than it began — but with no cut, no jolt, no
motion break. Just an organic forward drift of attention.

## STYLE CONSTANTS

50 mm cinema lens, f/2 character, six-blade hexagonal aperture. ACES
filmic color grade. Deep blacks at ~5 IRE (never crushed). 6 % film grain.
40 % corner vignette. HDR particle intensities so sharp cores naturally
bloom. Motion-control camera smoothness — absolute locked-off, zero
hand-held wobble.

NO panning. NO dolly. NO tilt. NO whip-pans. NO lens-flare streaks.
NO chrome. NO oversaturated colors (mood saturation max 50 %).
NO music-video cuts — this is one continuous shot.

The room always has **air**. The shot grows out of the attached first frame
— it does not start over.
