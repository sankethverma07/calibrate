# Calibrate — Master Kling Multi-Shot Prompt
## 28-second product film, all shots in one prompt

---

**Cinematic product film, 28 seconds, no audio, premium aesthetic.
Apple Vision Pro reveal × Samsung Galaxy Unpacked style.**

## THE WORLD (establish once, holds throughout)

The scene is set inside an ATMOSPHERIC AMBIENT ENVIRONMENT — not a black void,
not a flat color, but a deep cinematic stage with air and depth. Think the
inside of a Samsung Galaxy Unpacked cyclorama or the volumetric space of
Apple's Vision Pro reveal. There is a distant soft horizon, gentle volumetric
haze tinted by the current mood color, and barely-visible vertical light
shafts cutting through from somewhere above frame — like sun rays through
dust in a vast room. Corners of frame fade to slightly deeper tones than the
center, giving natural cinematographic vignette.

The hero subject in front of this environment is an "ocean of light" — a wide
horizontal plane filled with ten thousand microscopic glowing dust particles.
Each particle is a tiny photonic point, self-luminous, roughly the brightness
of a distant star. They are NOT fireflies, NOT bubbles, NOT water droplets —
they are pinpoint photons, like dust caught in a sunbeam but glowing from
within. The plane of particles undulates slowly like a calm sea on a smooth
multi-directional sine wave displacement field. Most particles cluster in the
lower half of frame; the upper half holds the atmospheric backdrop with its
mood-tinted haze.

Each particle renders with cinema-lens character. When in focus: a razor-sharp
cool-white pinpoint with a tiny 4-point diffraction spike (cinema iris
artifact). When out of focus: a hexagonal bokeh disc with bright "soap-bubble"
rim and subtle red/cyan chromatic fringing at the edge — six-blade aperture
iris (Cooke S4/i lens character), NOT a perfect circle, NOT a soft sprite.
The contrast between sharp pinpricks and creamy hexagonal bokeh is the central
cinematography signature.

Color temperature varies particle to particle — most are cool 5500–7000 Kelvin,
some are warmer 4000K cream-tone. The overall mood color shifts across four
states: teal `#2dd4bf` (Cruising), blue `#60a5fa` (Locked In), magenta
`#c084fc` (Drifting), orange `#fb923c` (Restless).

**The atmosphere shifts with mood** — slow 2-3 second crossfades, never snaps:

- **Cruising (teal):** soft deep teal cyclorama, like the inside of a cool
  calm room at dawn. Faint horizon line at the bottom third where the wave
  meets a distant haze. Subtle vertical light shafts cutting from above.
  Far edges of frame fade to deeper teal-indigo.

- **Locked In (blue):** looking up from beneath the surface of a deep ocean.
  Subtle caustic light-play overhead. Cool indigo gradient, darker at corners,
  slightly lighter at center. Volumetric haze gives the air weight. Distant
  motes of light flicker far away like deep-sea bioluminescence.

- **Drifting (magenta):** soft Northern-Lights / nebula aurora drapes the
  upper third of frame — slow-moving magenta-pink wash with hints of violet.
  Atmospheric haze tinted dreamy. The wave dust below catches the aurora's
  tint. Ethereal, slightly otherworldly.

- **Restless (orange):** warm sunset / ember glow at the horizon, smoke haze
  drifting through frame. Deep oranges and red-browns in the corners. The
  air feels charged, slightly hot. Light shafts more dynamic, like firelight.

All four atmospheres are DEEPLY DESATURATED and LOW LUMINANCE (~10-15% of
full brightness) so they never compete with the wave hero — but they're
clearly NOT BLACK. They give the camera depth to breathe in. Atmospheric
volumetric haze fills the air so there's always something for light to
catch.

The product is **Calibrate**, a smart equalizer that reads your focus state
and tunes audio. A frosted dark-glass control card with eight EQ sliders
(frequency bands 60, 170, 310, 600, 1K, 3K, 6K, 12K Hz) appears later in the
film, floating above the ocean. The card is translucent dark glass — NO
chrome, NO mirror reflections, NO clearcoat shine — pure frosted glass that
the wave and the atmosphere both read through.

## SEQUENCE

**0:00 – 0:03 · Open inside the ocean.** Camera locked-off, 50 cm above the
wave surface, tilted down 12 degrees, 50mm cinema lens at f/2. Wave fills lower
two thirds of centered frame. Focal plane DEEP in the field — far particles
razor-sharp pinpricks; foreground a wall of creamy hexagonal bokeh discs with
bright rims. Cruising teal mood. Particles drift gently.

**0:03 – 0:07 · Focus pulls forward.** Camera HOLDS POSITION — absolutely no
panning. The focal plane racks smoothly from 8 meters depth → 4 meters depth
across 4 seconds. The focal plane sweeps THROUGH the wave: background particles
defocus into creamy hexagons; midground particles crystallize into razor
pinpoints. Mood color seamlessly lerps teal → blue over 3 seconds. Wave
amplitude swells slightly.

**0:07 – 0:10 · Focus lands in the foreground.** Camera lifts 10 cm for subtle
parallax. Focal plane continues rack to 2 meters. Near foreground particles
become pixel-precise pinpoint stars with diffraction spikes; everything beyond
is creamy hexagonal bokeh filling 60% of frame. Macro-photography depth-of-field
aesthetic. Locked In blue mood settled. THIS IS THE MACRO-SHOT MONEY FRAME.

**0:10 – 0:12.5 · Slider handles rise.** Camera smoothly dollies back and lifts
to 1.2 m height. From the wave surface, eight bright orange pill-shaped slider
handles rise into view, one by one, 0.15 seconds staggered between each. Under
each handle, a thin dark recessed track capsule materializes. Tiny frequency
labels (60Hz, 170Hz, 310Hz, 600Hz, 1KHz, 3KHz, 6KHz, 12KHz) fade in below each
track in muted gray small caps. The wave continues drifting in soft bokeh below.

**0:12.5 – 0:15.5 · Slow-motion close-up: one slider drives the ocean.** SMASH
CUT to extreme macro close-up of a single slider handle (the 1 kHz band,
center-right of the row). 85mm cinema lens, razor-sharp focus on the metal
handle. Wave dust behind dissolves to 15% opacity. SLOW MOTION: the orange
handle rises smoothly from center position to +3 dB position over 2.5 seconds.
As it rises, the wave amplitude beneath that frequency band visibly BLOOMS UP
— particles in the corresponding region surge higher, creating a glowing crest
exactly under the slider. Mood color shifts to Drifting magenta during the
gesture, as if the user moving the slider also nudged their focus state.
Backlight halo on the handle.

**0:15.5 – 0:17.5 · All bands aligned.** Camera pulls back to medium shot
showing all 8 orange slider handles in a row with their dark tracks and
frequency labels. Wave breathes softly behind. Mood crossfades back toward
Locked In blue.

**0:17.5 – 0:20.5 · Glass card forms.** Camera continues dollying back. A
frosted dark glass card materializes around the eight sliders — translucent
body (72% opacity), heavy frost, NO chrome, NO mirror, NO clearcoat. Card has
orange "CUSTOM EQ" label top-left, white "Locked In preset" title in large
weight 300 sans-serif, four mood pill tabs top-right (Cruising / Locked In /
Drifting / Restless with Locked In highlighted), tracked-small-caps subtitle,
and two pill buttons bottom-right ("Save & apply", "Reset"). Card fades in via
opacity ramp over 2.5 seconds. The wave reads through the frosted glass at low
opacity.

**0:20.5 – 0:22.5 · Subtle orbit.** Camera slow lateral orbit 30 degrees around
the glass card. Frosted-glass refraction visible from new angle. Particles
behind cast a soft blue halo catching in the glass. Sliders and UI remain
crisply readable.

**0:22.5 – 0:24.5 · Held hero.** Camera locks back to front view, slightly
higher and farther. Glass card composed at rule-of-thirds upper third. Wave
breathes slowly in lower half. A single bright specular highlight slowly
travels left to right across the card's top edge over 2 seconds.

**0:24.5 – 0:28 · Title reveal.** Glass card and wave fade to pure black. The
text "Smart Equalizer." materializes letter by letter in 3D space — each letter
starts edge-on (rotated 90 degrees on the Y axis), pushed back in Z, tilted
slightly, scaled small, fully transparent. Then rotates and falls into final
upright position with a slight scale-overshoot bounce. 0.09 seconds staggered
between letters. Letters land in light off-white #f5f5f4 with a soft cool-blue
radial glow halation behind. After all 16 letters have settled (approximately
2.5 seconds), the subtitle "AUDIO THAT READS HOW YOU FOCUS" fades in below in
tracked small caps. Hold the final composition. Pure black background with
faint blue radial glow at 5% intensity.

## STYLE CONSTANTS

Cinema-lens aesthetic: hexagonal bokeh, six-blade aperture, ACES filmic color
grade, true 0 IRE black, 6% film grain, 40% corner vignette. Motion-control
camera smoothness — no hand-held wobble. Apple-level restraint, no music-video
maximalism. Premium product film. Macro photography depth-of-field references
(pink flower macro with one bloom sharp, rest creamy bokeh). NO lens flare
streaks. NO chrome reflections on glass. NO crushed-black banding. NO
oversaturated colors (mood saturation max 50%). NO mid-motion cuts (cut on
settles or luma matches only).
